The Problem

Juba's Plastic
Crisis at a Glance

The Nile River — primary water source for millions — is drowning in plastic. Cholera, malaria, flooding. Plastic discarded in Juba today flows downstream, threatening the Sudd wetlands — Africa's largest freshwater reserve — and ultimately reaching the Mediterranean Sea. South Sudan's only recycling plant shut down in 2016 and was never replaced. NILE FORGE is the last line of defense for the Nile ecosystem.

The Solution

From Waste
to Value

Modelled on Gjenge Makers Ltd (Nairobi) — which recycled 200,000+ kg of plastic and created 600 jobs — NILE FORGE adapts this proven model for Juba. No chemicals required. Solar-powered production makes every brick carbon-neutral. A digital traceability system (Nile-Trace) records each kilo of plastic from collection to final product — so donors can say exactly which 500 bottles their investment pulled from the Nile, who collected them, and which building they now form part of.

Standards & Quality

The Nile Brick —
Technical Specification

Construction firms and institutional buyers need verified confidence before adopting recycled materials. The data is unambiguous — Nile Bricks outperform conventional concrete across every measurable dimension. Based on benchmarks established by Gjenge Makers Ltd (Nairobi).

Feature Traditional Concrete NILE FORGE Plastic-Sand
Compressive Strength 12–15 MPa 8–140 MPa Up to 7× stronger
Weight ~2,200 kg/m³ ~950–1,050 kg/m³ 50% lighter
Water Absorption 5%–10% (porous) <1% Near waterproof
Melting Point N/A >350°C Safe in 40°C+ Juba heat
Lifespan 20–30 years 50+ years Fibrous & flexible
🏛️
Certification Plan — South Sudan Bureau of Standards
Phase 0 includes a technical audit in partnership with the South Sudan Bureau of Standards. The first 100 bricks produced will undergo compressive strength testing and fire-retardant testing before any product reaches market. Certification results will be published in our Phase 1 Mid-Term Report — giving procurement officers and construction firms a verifiable quality baseline they can cite in tenders and contracts.

Global Framework

UN Sustainable
Development Goals

NILE FORGE directly advances six of the United Nations' 17 Sustainable Development Goals — making it eligible for SDG-linked grant programmes, impact bonds, and ESG-mandated institutional funds.

SDG 6
Clean Water & Sanitation
Removing plastic from the Nile directly protects the water quality relied on by millions daily. Improved drainage prevents contamination of informal settlement water points during seasonal flooding.
SDG 8
Decent Work & Economic Growth
Formalising the informal waste sector — providing Environmental Technicians with PPE, fair wages, health insurance, and mobile banking. Green Jobs for Juba's most marginalised youth and women.
SDG 9
Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure
Creating a circular economy industrial unit that manufactures superior construction materials locally — replacing costly imported inputs with solar-powered, certified alternatives produced in South Sudan.
SDG 11
Sustainable Cities & Communities
Reducing Juba's urban waste burden and providing affordable, durable materials for low-cost housing and public spaces — while clearing plastic-clogged drains that cause seasonal flash flooding in informal settlements.
SDG 12
Responsible Consumption & Production
The core of NILE FORGE is Waste-to-Value. Ensuring plastic is reused as a structural asset — certified bricks and premium furniture — rather than burned, dumped, or washed into the Nile and Sudd.
SDG 13
Climate Action
Solar-powered production eliminates diesel. Each Nile Brick prevents an estimated 2.4 kg of CO₂ versus conventional concrete — qualifying NILE FORGE for Green Climate Fund mandates and carbon credit revenue.
